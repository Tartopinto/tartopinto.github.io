My research focuses on the **Statistical Physics of Active Matter** and the development of high-performance numerical frameworks for out-of-equilibrium systems.

---

## 🌊 Active Matter & Phase Separation
Most of my work explores how "active" particles (those that consume energy to move) organize themselves into complex macroscopic patterns.

### Bubbly Phase Separation (BPS)
I have extensively studied the **Active Model B+ (AMB+)**, where active fluids undergo phase separation characterized by the emergence of steady-state bubbles.
* **Numerical Implementation:** Custom-built solvers in **C** using parallelised pseudo-spectral methods for 2D density fields.
* **Large-Scale Data:** Managing and analyzing terabyte-scale simulation outputs (>1 TB) to extract quantitative insights.

> **Key Achievement:** Demonstrated the existence of self-organized critical coexistence phases in repulsive active particles, published in *Physical Review Letters*.

---

## 🛠️ Computational Expertise
My research is grounded in a strong "under-the-hood" approach to physics:
* **Algorithms:** Pseudo-spectral and finite differences schemes for PDEs and stochastic integration for SDEs.
* **HPC:** Parallelization using **OpenMP** for intensive CPU workloads.
* **Data Pipelines:** Advanced data processing with **Python (NumPy, SciPy)** and Bash scripting.

---

## 🚀 Future Directions: Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)
Building on my background in numerical integration, I am currently exploring the transition toward **Scientific Machine Learning**.
* **Objective:** Investigating how Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and neural operators can accelerate or augment traditional SDE/PDE solvers.
* **Focus:** Maintaining physical symmetries and conservation laws within machine learning architectures.
