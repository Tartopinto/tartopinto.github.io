My research lies at the intersection of **Statistical Physics**, **High-Performance Computing**, and **Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)**. I develop numerical frameworks to study the emergent properties of out-of-equilibrium systems, specifically focusing on active matter and phase separation.

---

## 🔬 Current Focus: Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)
I am currently transitioning my expertise in stochastic differential equations (SDEs) and PDEs toward **Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)** and surrogate modeling.
* **Goal:** Accelerate complex physical simulations by replacing expensive numerical integrators with neural operators.
* **Approach:** Leveraging symmetry-preserving architectures to ensure that AI models respect fundamental physical laws (conservation of mass, momentum).

---

## 🌊 Active Matter & Phase Separation
A significant part of my work explores how "active" particles (those that consume energy to move) organize themselves into complex patterns.

### Bubbly Phase Separation (BPS)
I have extensively studied the **BPS model**, where active fluids undergo phase separation with the emergence of steady-state bubbles.
* **Numerical Implementation:** Custom-built solvers in **C** using pseudo-spectral methods for 2D density fields.
* **HPC Optimization:** Parallelized code with **OpenMP**, managing simulations on terabyte-scale datasets (>1 TB).

<div class="notice--info">
  <h4>Key Achievement</h4>
  <p>Demonstrated the existence of self-organized critical coexistence phases in repulsive active particles, published in <em>Physical Review Letters</em>.</p>
</div>
